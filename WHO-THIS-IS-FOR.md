# Who this is for

This harness is built for people who treat AI-assisted engineering as engineering — not as a vibes-driven productivity hack.

## Target audiences

- **Solo senior engineers** who carry the same standards across projects, contracts, and roles, and don't want to rewrite their tribal wisdom every time they open a new repo.
- **AI-native leads** building or scaling teams whose default workflow includes coding agents, and who need shared doctrine before the team accumulates contradictory conventions.
- **Consulting teams** who land in unfamiliar codebases and want a portable operating layer — review rubrics, agent skills, security posture — that travels with them.
- **Internal platform and DevEx teams** standardizing AI-assisted development across an organization, who need a written baseline that survives staff changes.
- **Engineering managers** trying to establish "this is how we use AI" before that decision is made for them by whichever team member shouts loudest.

## What you get

A doctrine library you fork, curate, and own:

- Engineering, architecture, delivery, and AI-agent operating principles.
- Coding standards for PHP/Laravel, Python, TypeScript/Node, and SQL.
- Cloud and delivery rules for Docker, Kubernetes, CI/CD, and observability.
- Security and supply-chain policies, including AI-specific risks and a GDPR posture.
- Review rubrics for code and architecture.
- Agent skills (code review, architecture review, test design, Docker hardening) with a canonical SKILL format.
- Tool adapters: a deep Claude reference adapter, plus lighter sketches for Cursor, Copilot, Codex, and generic `AGENTS.md`.

## Not for you if

- You want a runnable productivity tool. The harness is text. Nothing here generates, validates, or executes anything.
- You want a single dogmatic answer to architecture, framework, or stack choice. The harness deliberately presents patterns as peer options and refuses to pick a default.
- You want a finished product. The harness is a *starting point*. Forkers are expected to delete what doesn't fit, rewrite what does, and shape it into their own version.
- You want the doctrine to evolve with you upstream. The contribution model is "fork it and curate." See [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](./CONTRIBUTING.md).
- You're looking for a prompt zoo. There are no clever prompts here.
